Trail-tested notes

Experience summary

I built this guide around field photography sessions on Mount Passaconaway in Sandwich Range. Mount Passaconaway is a 4,043-foot mountain in the Sandwich Range, located in Grafton County (town of Waterville Valley), New Hampshire.

Conditions from experience

Summer and fall - Fall foliage from ledges; winter doable but Blueberry Ledge can be icy. Low - mostly sheltered; caution on open ledges in rain/ice.

Planning tip

Dicey’s Mill Trail - standard ascent. - 9.2 - 2,950 - Difficult - Out & back - or loop with Blueberry Ledge. Yes - Wonalancet River at Dicey’s Mill crossing ~1.7 mi in; small brook on lower Blueberry Ledge; none high up. Ferncroft lot is large grassy area for hikers; free, fills on nice weekends with hikers for Whiteface/Passaconaway
