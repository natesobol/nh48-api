Trail-tested notes

Experience summary

I built this guide around field photography sessions on Mount Carrigain in Pemigewasset Wilderness - Carrigain.. Mount Carrigain is a 4,700-foot mountain in the White Mountains of New Hampshire, located in Grafton County.

Conditions from experience

Summer and fall - Tower views best on clear days; Sawyer River Road closed in winter. Medium - exposed ridge and tower; can be windy, but much of trail is in woods.

Planning tip

Signal Ridge Trail - out & back. - 10.0 - 3,250 - Difficult - Out & back Yes - Whiteface Brook near trail start; small streams along lower Signal Ridge Trail. Small lot off Sawyer River Road - road is gated in winter ~2 miles from trailhead.
